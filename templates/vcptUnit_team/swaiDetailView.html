Kyeba Swai

Study Coordinator

Kyeba Swai is a result-driven medical entomologist with ten years of accumulated experience in conducting and supervising semi-field and field studies collecting entomological data and its analysis. He is currently registered for a PhD and works at the Vector Control Product Testing Unit (VCPTU) primarily leading the evaluations on spatial repellents but also supports phase II (experimental hut) evaluations of long-lasting insecticidal nets. Before joining VCPTU, he led several research projects in south eastern Tanzania including, a malaria prevalence survey across several villages of Ulanga district and on a project that aimed to develop low-cost and portable mosquito-proof houses to protect migratory rice farmers from mosquito-borne illnesses. He is interested in the control of vector borne diseases, specifically in understanding the role of migratory livelihoods in maintaining the transmission of malaria. He is keen on developing research agendas that incorporate this aspect as well as the development and evaluation of novel mosquito control tools, particularly those that can be used by migratoryÂ communities.
